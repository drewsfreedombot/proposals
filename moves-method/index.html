What We Heard

Key pain points and opportunities from our conversations with Amanda and Daniel

🤖 AI Conversation Quality is #1 Priority

Amanda's biggest frustration with existing AI tools is they sound robotic — "like speaking to an underpaid VA." Charlie's casual, natural conversation style was what caught her eye immediately. With 40 setters + 17 dialers, conversation quality at scale is everything. ManyChat "dropped the ball" — Charlie's four-judge AI course correction system is the differentiator.

📞 Reduce 57-Person Team, Not Replace

At peak, Moves Method had 75 setters. Currently 40 setters + 17 outbound dialers = 57 people. The goal is to reduce that team by layering in AI for the repetitive volume — text-based outreach at scale while human setters focus on phone calls and high-value conversations. Amanda explicitly said: "more tech, less user error."

💬 Dropped Conversations on Handoff

When a lead transfers from a setter to a closer, the conversation reassigns. Setters can't see it anymore, incoming messages fall into a void. Amanda needs a bridge agent to catch orphaned messages and facilitate rescheduling. Kill switch capability is critical — Amanda and Daniel both flagged this from their ManyChat experience.

📱 Instagram DM at Scale (1M+ Followers)

Amanda used to manage an IGDM team — knows the channel intimately. ManyChat handles new follower initial messages but drops conversations when things go off-script. Charlie handles everything after ManyChat's initial trigger: keyword DMs, comment replies, and full conversational follow-up. ManyChat stays for new follower outbound only.

🔧 Dual-CRM Technical Complexity

Moved to HubSpot ~3 months ago (140K contacts), but GHL still holds ~400K contacts. Proper reactivation requires working both systems. Daniel flagged: (1) carrier blocking risk on mass sends, (2) international SMS costs (~30% non-US leads), (3) conversation visibility for setters across platforms. Solution: Charlie integrates natively with both HubSpot and GHL, batched sends, WhatsApp for international. Note: Amanda mentioned they recently did webinar-based reactivations — so the team has some reactivation experience already.
