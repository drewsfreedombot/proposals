Discovery Summary

What We Heard

Key pain points and opportunities from our conversation

🤖 AI Conversation Quality

Amanda's biggest frustration with existing AI tools (ChatGPT, chatbots) is that they sound robotic — "like speaking to an underpaid VA." Charlie's casual, natural conversation style was the #1 thing that caught her eye on the website. The AI needs to sound human and match the brand's energetic, approachable tone.

📞 Outbound Dialing Team — Reduce, Not Replace

Moves Method has an outbound dialing team. The goal is NOT to fully replace them but to reduce the team size by layering in AI outreach — more volume, more consistency, lower cost per lead touched. Charlie handles the repetitive outreach at scale while human setters focus on high-value conversations.

💬 Dropped Conversations When Leads Transfer

When a lead is transferred from a setter to a closer, the conversation reassigns in the system. The setter can't see it anymore, and incoming messages from the lead fall into a void — nobody picks them up. Amanda needs a "bridge agent" that catches these orphaned messages and facilitates rescheduling or re-engagement so no lead falls through the cracks.

📱 Instagram DM Setting

With 1M+ followers and a massive social presence, Amanda wants to explore Instagram DM as a setting channel. Charlie can respond to IG DMs in real time — qualifying leads conversationally and booking calls 24/7 without the setter team needing to monitor the inbox.

🔗 Multi-System Integration

Amanda wants AI across multiple systems — not just one channel. HubSpot for CRM, Allaware for dialing, Instagram for DMs. Charlie integrates natively with HubSpot and Instagram, and can augment the Allaware workflow with SMS/messaging follow-up around dial attempts.

That's my biggest complaint. I work with ChatGPT all day and it's like speaking to an underpaid VA. If it's as casual as it seems like the videos on the site — that would be fantastic.

— Amanda Smith, Set Team Director

What We're Building for You

Phase 1 — Recovery agents that generate ROI immediately and create a safe training environment

These agents target existing leads and recovery opportunities. They generate ROI immediately — and they create a live, low-risk environment to train Charlie with real conversations before touching fresh top-of-funnel leads. Your setter team doesn't burn new leads while we optimize.

🔄 Agent 1 — Conversation Bridge / Handoff Recovery

The gap Amanda described — when leads transfer to closers and messages get dropped. This agent monitors for orphaned inbound messages post-transfer. When a lead sends a message that nobody's picking up, Charlie jumps in: confirms the appointment, answers basic questions, or facilitates rescheduling. Impersonates the closer's tone and keeps the conversation alive until the closer picks it up. No more leads falling through the cracks during handoffs.

Handoff Recovery Closer Impersonation Rescheduling HubSpot Integration

📲 Agent 2 — Show Rate Protector (7-Step Post-Booking)

Five minutes after a prospect books a call with a closer, Charlie texts from the closer's number: "Hey, it's [closer name]. Looking forward to our call. Just a heads up, we don't allow our schedule to stay open — if you need to reschedule, let me know now." Then drops into pre-call material, confirms they've reviewed it, and sends personal reminders leading up to the call. This is the single biggest ROI lever — pushing show rates toward 70–80% on cold, non-organic leads.

📞 Agent 5 — Outbound SMS/Text Setter (Dialing Team Augmentation)

This is the play to reduce the dialing team. Instead of human setters cold-calling every lead on the list, Charlie reaches out via SMS first — a warm, conversational text that feels personal. Leads who respond positively get qualified and booked. Leads who don't respond get flagged for a human dial attempt. This flips the model: AI handles the volume, humans handle the high-value conversations. Result: smaller dialing team, more booked calls, lower cost per appointment.

🎨 Brand Voice & Persona

  • Tone: Energetic, casual, approachable — matches the Moves Method brand. Not corporate, not salesy. Like a knowledgeable friend who genuinely wants to help.
  • Language style: Conversational and natural — the #1 thing Amanda flagged. Charlie must NOT sound like "an underpaid VA." Short sentences, natural flow, real personality.
  • Audience: Broad — from pro athletes to grandparents. Ages 20s–70s+. Communication needs to be warm and inclusive without being patronizing.
  • Congruency goal: Charlie's messaging should feel like it came from the same team that created the Moves Method content — same energy, same care, same approachability.
  • Custom persona: Available — provide 3–5 written back-and-forth messaging conversations by the persona you want Charlie to adopt. These must be written out (not video/call recordings). We'll use these to mirror the exact voice and personality.
  • Company website: Please share your best landing page or website with program/company information — we'll feed this into Charlie's knowledge base

⚙️ Technical Integration

  • CRM: HubSpot — deal stages updated automatically when Charlie books or qualifies. Agent triggers driven by pipeline stages and contact properties.
  • SMS: Confirm SMS provider — Twilio through HubSpot? Or separate provider behind Allaware? A2P registration status needed.
  • Instagram DM: Connected through Charlie for real-time inbound DM handling.
  • Allaware (Dialer): Charlie augments the dialing workflow — SMS outreach before/after dial attempts. Confirm integration capabilities on kickoff.
  • Calendar: Confirm booking system — HubSpot meetings, Calendly, or other?

🧠 Knowledge Base (What We'll Feed Charlie)

  • movesmethod.com — full program details, pricing, FAQs, the 3-step system
  • High-ticket coaching program details — what's included, pricing, delivery format, ideal client profile
  • Success stories and testimonials — 57,000+ customers across 50+ countries
  • Qualification criteria — what makes someone a fit for high-ticket coaching vs. low-ticket toolkit only
  • Objection handling — common pushbacks (pricing, time commitment, injury concerns, age-related doubts)

🔇 Knowledge Guardrails

  • Smart enough to be dangerous, not an expert: Charlie should NOT give specific mobility advice, exercise prescriptions, or injury assessments.   • Medical boundaries: Charlie must never provide medical advice, diagnose conditions, or make claims about treating injuries. Warm referral to the coaching team for anything specific.
