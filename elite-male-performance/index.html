Audit Summary

What Happened with Levrg

A transparent assessment of the previous build and what needs to change

🔴 Only 1–2 Agents Built (Should Be ~7)

Levrg built a single agent where there should be specialized agents for each stage of the funnel. Different funnel stages require different talk tracks, different tones, and different objectives. A one-size-fits-all agent can't handle the nuance of reactivation vs. top-of-funnel vs. post-booking vs. no-show recovery. This is red flag number one.

🔴 No Post-Booking Flow

The booking part was working — 30–40% lead-to-booking rate, solid conversation quality. Then it fell apart because there was zero post-booking follow-up. No closer impersonation, no pre-call confirmation, no show-rate optimization. Result: terrible show rate → kill switch. The booking engine was good; the delivery infrastructure didn't exist.

🔴 No Agent Instructions

The agents had no instructions configured — no "don't accept surface level answers," no "dive deeper," no conversational guardrails. Instructions are the core brain of an agent. Without them, Charlie operates generic and unfocused. Kyle ended up spending hours giving per-message feedback and rewriting Levrg's work to compensate.

🔴 Old Instance, Not Upgraded

The account is running on an older version of the Charlie platform. Knowledge base, settings UI, and agent capabilities have evolved. The old instance doesn't reflect current best practices or feature set. Needs to be brought up to current.

🔴 Document Upload Issues

Kyle reported that document uploads consistently fail. This may be related to the old instance. Regardless, direct document uploads are not the recommended approach for training — custom personas and managed knowledge base configuration produce better, more controllable results.

It was 50% perfect, and then it just obviously wasn't — they didn't connect it up. So as long as it can continue the same degree of quality for the post-booking flow, then I would have been stoked with it.

— Kyle Musca, Discovery Call

⚠️ Our Approach: Don't Trust, Verify

We're not going to trust what Levrg built. We have a headstart — not a rebuild — but we need to run through our full process. The existing setup will be audited, the agents will be rebuilt to spec, and we'll layer in our proven health/fitness/nutrition templates. Kyle's existing training assets (50 real conversations, tonality docs) will be incorporated properly through our managed build process.

Configuration Details

How We'll Configure Charlie

Technical setup, knowledge management, and dual-market architecture

🌏 Dual-Market Architecture (Australia + US)

  • Separate Twilio numbers: Australian leads get an Australian phone number. US leads get a US phone number. No cross-market confusion.
  • Separate agents per market: Each geography gets its own set of agents with localized language, metrics (kg vs lbs, etc.), and cultural tone.
  • Pipeline routing: GHL pipelines or tags divide leads by geography. Different pipeline stages trigger different agents for each market.
  • Charlie adapts conversationally: Charlie is smart enough to match, mirror, and adjust tonality to who he's talking to — but the structural routing (which agent, which number) is driven by CRM setup.

🧠 Knowledge Base Philosophy

  • Smart enough to be dangerous, not an expert: Charlie should NOT answer specific program questions (nutrition plans, keto vs carb-heavy, workout protocols). If asked, Charlie deflects: "Honestly, I wish I could answer that — I'm not the expert. But I know Kyle's programs are all built bespoke. Why don't we get you on a call and you can ask him directly?"
  • Settings override everything: Central settings (instructions, persona, personality, banned phrases) override any documents or feedback. This hierarchy is critical and non-negotiable.
  • No DIY document uploads: Kyle's 50 real conversations and tonality documents will be parsed by our team and incorporated through the managed build process — not uploaded directly. Direct uploads can create conflicts with settings and lead to unpredictable behavior.
  • Custom persona: Built from 3–5 written back-and-forth messaging conversations by the persona you want Charlie to adopt — these must be written out (not video/call recordings). Combined with Kyle's 50 real conversations, Charlie will sound like Kyle — his style, speech patterns, analogies, energy.
  • Company website: Please share your best landing page or website with program/company information — we'll feed this into Charlie's knowledge base

⚙️ Technical Integration

  • CRM: GoHighLevel — all agent triggers driven by pipeline stages, deal statuses, and contact tags
  • SMS: Twilio (already in place) — separate AU and US numbers
  • Pipeline-driven activation: New lead → TOF setter. Booked → show rate sequence. No-show → rebooking agent. Cancelled → cancellation agent. Dead → reactivation/scholarship/magic hat.
  • Data persistence: Charlie pushes conversation data into GHL (goals, hurdles, objections). This data persists and is used in future reactivation touchpoints for personalized follow-up.
  • CSV imports: Old leads imported via GHL, tagged/pipelined appropriately for reactivation campaigns

📊 Weekly Optimization (Ongoing)

  • Weekly data review: intro response rate, connect rate, book rate, show rate
  • Dynamic coaching: "Hey Kyle, I was looking at conversations — I think we need to push harder here" or "Let's dial back the intensity on this segment"
  • A/B testing messaging approaches across different agents
  • Weekly optimization workshops available (marketing, funnels, Charlie, selling)
  • Goal: Get agents to 10/10, then your job is to not touch them. Like a magic marketing campaign that's crushing — don't mess with it.
