Discovery Summary

What We Heard

The key challenges and opportunities you shared with us

📉 The Comment-to-Contact Bottleneck

This is your #1 constraint. You're generating hundreds of comments per post — people actively raising their hand for freebies and resources. ManyChat sends them the link, but there's a massive drop-off between commenting and actually clicking/downloading/giving contact info. Your VA follows up manually, but she's working through a constant backlog and can't keep pace with the volume. Charlie responds in under 60 seconds, follows up persistently, and has natural conversations that move people from "send me the video" to "here's my email, book me on a call" — without it feeling like a form fill.

🤖 VA Can't Scale with Your Content Volume

You post daily, and some posts generate couple thousand comments. Your VA is working through a backlog that keeps growing. The follow-up work — "did you watch the video? need any help? want a call?" — is low-value for a human but critical for conversion. Charlie handles this at infinite scale, following up on every single commenter with personalized, conversational messages that feel like Julia's team reaching out — not an automated sequence.

✅ Show Rate Is Already Strong — 80%+

Once someone books a call, they show up. This tells us your audience is highly motivated and your pre-call process works well. The opportunity isn't getting more people to show up — it's getting more people booked in the first place. That said, a show rate agent is available and free when you're ready. As volume increases from Charlie converting more commenters, maintaining that 80%+ becomes more important.

📊 High 20s/Low 30s Close Rate — Reactivation Opportunity

A 25-30% close rate means 70% of people who get on a call don't close. Charlie captures their goals and pain points during the initial conversation and pushes them to GHL as custom fields. When a closer moves a deal to dead, Charlie can follow up 60 days later with full context: "Last time we spoke, you were looking to hit a million points — how's that coming along?" This contextual reactivation consistently outperforms generic spray-and-pray outreach.

🏷️ "Bad Fit" Leads Are Future Revenue

You mentioned tagging leads as "bad fit" for 1:1 or group coaching — but wanting to reactivate them later when you launch a low-ticket course or new offer. Charlie stores all conversation context and can reach back out to those tagged leads when you're ready, with a tailored message about the new offer. No lead is wasted.

The bottleneck is the booking rate. We have so many people who are engaging with the Instagram content. But as soon as they're like, oh, I got to give my phone number, I'm not doing it.

Configuration Details

How We'll Configure Charlie

Technical and brand setup details — please confirm or correct anything that needs updating

🎨 Brand Voice & Persona

  • Persona: Charlie shows up as Julia's assistant — knowledgeable, helpful, and approachable. Ideally mirrors Julia's voice and personality since she's the face of the brand.
  • Tone: Friendly, knowledgeable, travel-enthusiast energy — matches the excitement your audience feels about points and miles
  • Custom persona (optional): Provide video content of Julia and we'll build a persona that mirrors her communication style
  • Key behavior: Charlie should NOT over-educate — if asked about strategy or methodology, redirect to the call: "Great question — that's exactly what we'd cover on a call with our team." Keep them curious, not satisfied.

⚙️ Technical Integration

  • CRM: GoHighLevel — deal stages updated automatically. Charlie moves qualified leads to "Booked," dead leads to reactivation pipeline.
  • Instagram DM: Charlie handles all comment-triggered DMs natively. Replaces ManyChat for comments.
  • ManyChat: Keep for new follower outbound ONLY — Charlie can't natively outbound to new followers. ManyChat triggers the intro, Charlie takes over the conversation.
  • SMS via GHL: For reactivation and show rate agents. A2P likely active (verify on kickoff).
  • Calendar: Booking via calendar link or conversational booking — we'll A/B test both to see which converts better for your audience.
  • Qualification: Conversational qualification by Charlie, with GHL automations routing disqualified leads to setter calendar and qualified leads to closers.
  • Time-bound messaging: Charlie configured to respect business hours for outbound — no 2 AM texts to US leads from overnight ad responses.

🧠 Knowledge Base (What We'll Feed Charlie)

  • geobreezetravel.com — program details, offer tiers, positioning
  • Julia's bio and background — actuary, points expertise, credibility markers
  • Lead magnet details — what each freebie covers (credit card consults, award search videos, newsletter) so Charlie can reference them naturally
  • Qualification criteria — your calculator variables (points balance, type, card spend) translated into conversational questions
  • Disqualification criteria — non-US leads, financial thresholds, bad fit indicators
  • Common objections and FAQ — what prospects push back on
  • Example conversations that converted well — so Charlie learns from what's working

📊 Data Flow & Custom Fields

  • Goal capture: When Charlie surfaces a prospect's goal ("I want to fly business class to Tokyo on points"), it's pushed to a GHL custom field
  • Hurdle capture: When Charlie uncovers the blocker ("I have 500K points but don't know how to use them for international flights"), pushed to a separate field
  • Why this matters: Every downstream agent — reactivation, post-call follow-up, low-ticket reactivation — references these fields for personalized outreach
  • "Bad fit" tagging: Disqualified leads tagged and stored for future low-ticket reactivation when you're ready
